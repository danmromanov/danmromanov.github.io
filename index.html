Daniil Romanov

DR

My research spans public opinion, the political economy of technology and their regulation, political institutions in both democratic and authoritarian contexts, political communication, and international studies. At the core of this agenda lies a sustained interest in how digital technologies — social media platforms, messaging applications, AI, and censorship circumvention tools — are deployed by political actors, reshape political processes, and shape the beliefs and attitudes of citizens.

Alongside this, I maintain a sustained research interest in the politics of autocratisation and democratisation, and in the study of political institutions — in particular elections. I also pursue research on political communication, with a particular focus on propaganda and its effects on public opinion.

Geographically, my work has centred on Russia and China, though I have increasingly turned to international studies, including questions of how authoritarian states influence one another and project that influence into Western European and North American democracies. Methodologically, I draw on survey experiments (conjoint, vignette, list designs), large-scale digital trace data, text-as-data approaches, and geospatial data.

Over the course of a five-year academic career, I have published or had conditionally accepted 11 papers in leading political science journals.
